About

alex's logo here

AHPMUN (American Heritage Plantation Model United Nations) is a high school Model UN conference held at American Heritage High School (Plantation) from September 29-30, 2018. For the second time, AHPMUN is open to non-Heritage students, presenting delegates with an opportunity to engage in lively, competitive debate. The Secretariat is composed of students from American Heritage's nationally ranked Model United Nations Team.

Keynote Speaker

Jude Alawa

Jude Alawa recently graduated from Yale University as a Global Health Scholar majoring in Global Affairs and Molecular, Cellular, and Developmental Biology. He is from Miami, Florida by way of Damascus, Syria and is interested in the intersection between medicine and international relations, specifically with respect to humanitarian assistance and access to healthcare among marginalized populations. Jude has worked for the National Health Service in London evaluating and implementing new models of community healthcare, for the Council on Foreign Relations researching domestic and global health policy, and for Pharos Global Health devising appropriate interventions to address international health disparities. Jude’s current work focuses on providing water purification devices to Syrian refugee camps and addressing interventions to improve chronic disease care among refugees. In the future, Jude hopes to study and craft effective measures to improve clinical standards and increase access to healthcare in under-resourced communities at home and abroad. At Yale, Jude was active in the Yale Rotaract Club (Local/International Service), the Yale International Relations Association, Instrumental Connection (Music Instruction), and as a research assistant at the Yale Medical School. Next year, Jude will be pursuing his Masters in Public Health at the University of Cambridge as a Rotary Scholar and a Fox Fellow.

During Jude Alawa's Academic Career

He was a 2015 graduate of American Heritage and was actively involved in Model UN since his freshman year.

He was President of Model UN at AHS in 2014-2015, Vice President as a junior in 2013-2014, and won numerous Best Delegate Awards in his high school career.

He was President of National Honor Society during his senior year at NHS and was named Outstanding Senior in 2015.

He was actively involved in Model UN at Yale. He was Director-General of YMUN in January 2017, and he was USG of Yale Model UN Budapest as well.
